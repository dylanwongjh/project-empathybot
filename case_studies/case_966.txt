=== CASE STUDY PROFILE ===
Topic: Caregiver Burnout, Anticipatory Grief, Entrepreneurial Anxiety

=== CLINICAL METADATA ===
Caregiver Profile: A 30-40-year-old entrepreneur with a strong desire to help others, possibly in a caregiving or advocacy role.
Clinical Goal: To address the client's feelings of overwhelm, burnout, and anxiety related to starting a new business and potentially launching a support group.
Summary: An entrepreneur struggling to balance work responsibilities while considering starting a support group for individuals with similar experiences, feeling overwhelmed by the potential challenges and emotional toll.

=== PATIENT EMOTIONAL PROFILE ===
Primary Emotions: Overwhelm, Anxiety, Guilt
Cognitive State: Catastrophizing, Flooding
Underlying Need: Emotional safety and control

=== COMMUNICATION TECHNIQUES USED ===
- Active listening (acknowledging the client's feelings and concerns)
- Validation (recognizing the client's emotions as normal and understandable)
- Reframing (helping the client see potential solutions or alternatives to their problems)
- Empathy (showing understanding of the client's desire to help others while acknowledging the challenges involved)
