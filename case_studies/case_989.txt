Topic: Anticipatory grief and caregiver burnout

Caregiver Profile: Adult child of a terminally ill parent, likely in their 40s or 50s
Clinical Goal: To help the client process guilt and shame related to past caregiving decisions, explore alternative coping mechanisms, and identify resources for managing caregiver burden.
Summary: A middle-aged adult child is grappling with long-held feelings of guilt and responsibility for not being able to alleviate their terminally ill mother's suffering during a difficult time in their childhood.

=== PATIENT EMOTIONAL PROFILE ===
Primary Emotions: Guilt, Helplessness, Sadness
Cognitive State: Catastrophizing, Rumination
Underlying Need: Emotional safety and control

=== COMMUNICATION TECHNIQUES USED ===
Active listening, Validation, Reframing (acknowledging the client's feelings while also providing a more nuanced perspective), Empathic exploration of past experiences.
